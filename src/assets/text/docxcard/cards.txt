Front: What are the two main groups in which the animal kingdom is divided?
Back: The two main groups in which the animal kingdom is divided are invertebrates and vertebrates.

Front: What percentage of all animals do invertebrates make up?
Back: Invertebrates make up about 98% of all animals.

Front: What are some characteristics that all animals share?
Back: All animals are multicellular eukaryotes without cell walls and can have different body plans and symmetry, as well as a gut, body cavity, and differentiated tissues.

Front: What are some phyla within the animal kingdom?
Back: There are various phyla within the animal kingdom, including Porifera, Cnidaria, Platyhelminthes, Annelida, Mollusca, Arthropoda, Echinodermata, and Chordata.

Front: What are the three subphyla within Chordata?
Back: The three subphyla within Chordata are Tunicata, Cephalochordata, and Vertebrata.

Front: What is the characteristic of vertebrates and how are they classified?
Back: Vertebrates have a backbone or notochord and are classified into different classes, including Agnatha, Osteichthyes, Chondrichthyes, Amphibia, Reptilia, Aves, and Mammalia.

Front: What are some characteristics of mammals?
Back: Mammals are a type of vertebrate and have certain characteristics such as being endothermic, tetrapod, (mostly) viviparous, having four-chambered hearts, hair/fur, and mammary glands.
